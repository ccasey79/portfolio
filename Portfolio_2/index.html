About

I've always been involved in technology throughout my career, working originally within the post-production and broadcasting industries. In 2016 I diversed into web development and attended a bootcamp course which gave me a great insight into how to build websites. I moved into web development as I really enjoy the variety of work, each project has it's unique differences. There's always opportunities to learn something new and it doesn't become monotonous in it's processes. Working in web development allows me to research into new technologies, try out new techniquies and become creative with the designs and processes.

The course I did at General Assembly gave me a great exposure into learning new technologies. Working in teams and as an individual gave me excellent experience into pair programming and programming fundamentals. Such technologies I gained an understanding of for example are Node.js, version control using Git, using API's and using Frameworks incorporating MVC with Angular and Ruby on Rails.

I'm now currently working for DX2, have been working on a range of client projects. The role is very multi-tasked for example I could be changing designs with Adobe Photoshop, coding with HTML, CSS or jQuery or using up to date SEO practices for particular sites. Working here I've felt I've learnt so much and gained valuable experience. The first four projects below in the portfolio section are examples of my work with DX2.

If I'm not working with code I love travelling particulary to slightly more unusual places like Iceland and Cuba which I have recently visited. I also have a big interest in films, I am regularly at the cinema and especially enjoy films by Wes Anderson and Paul Thomas Anderson.
